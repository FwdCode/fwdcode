FAQ

What is FwdCode?
FwdCode is a college hackathon promoting unity and social good. Each event is 24 hours long and will be open to college students across the U.S.

What even is a hackathon anyway?
I'm glad you asked! A hackathon is basically a coding competition, where coders come to create projects and work together. At FwdCode, our hackathon will also include workshops, networking opportunity, prizes, swag, and more!

Where will FwdCode be held?
Even FwdCode event will be held at a different college or university. Stay tuned for the location of the first event!

When will FwdCode be held?
The first FwdCode event will be held in Fall 2017. Stay tuned for the exact dates of the event!

What do I need to bring to FwdCode?
The only requirements are a computer, any charging equipment you may need, and yourself! Many hackers also like to bring a sleeping back and/or pillow (there usually isn't much sleeping at a hackathon, but some hackers like to take a nap or two!), toothbrush/toothpaste, or a change of clothes (if you want to freshen up). If you plan on doing any hardware hacking, you need to bring all of the materials you're going to use for that.

What will be provided?
We will provide three meals (lunch and dinner on the first day and breakfast on the second) as well as abundant snacks and drinks. If you choose not to eat the food we provide, you're responsible for getting your own meals (unless you have an allergy or dietary restriction, which we will accomodate -- just let us know!). We'll also provide outlets, t-shirts, swag, and wifi. If you have any specific questions about what will be provided please email us at hello@fwdcode.org.

What are the requirements to attend?
The only requirement to attend is that you are a college student. You don't need to have any coding or hackathon experience -- just a willingness to collaborate and create!

Can I attend if I'm in high school?
Maybe! Email us at hello@fwdcode.org if you're in high school and would like to attend.

Will you provide travel reimbursements?
Maybe! We'll release more information on that when we can.
